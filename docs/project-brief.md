# Project Brief

## Purpose

Lounge Coffee is a fictional premium coffee brand created as a fast, visually ambitious landing page concept. The project is a personal sprint focused on building a product-storytelling website that feels cinematic, intentional, and premium without turning into a full ecommerce platform.

## Brand Fiction

### Product

`Locked`: v1 presents Lounge Coffee as a premium takeaway coffee line with multiple cup variants rather than a café locator or a packaged bean store.

### What "Lounge" Means

`Locked`: the brand tone should combine calm ritual with confident energy. It should feel warm, composed, slightly indulgent, and modern rather than rustic or overly playful.

### Audience

`Preferred`: design-conscious coffee drinkers who respond to premium visual presentation, modern lifestyle branding, and product-led storytelling.

### Emotional Promise

`Locked`: the page is selling freshness, aroma, craft, and desire. It should make the product feel elevated and memorable before it explains anything practical.

## Brand Feel

- Premium
- Warm
- Rich
- Immersive
- Alive
- Playful but polished
- Cinematic
- Product-focused
- Storytelling-driven

## Creative Translation Rules

- `Locked`: premium means restrained copy, clear focal hierarchy, strong negative space, and no novelty motion
- `Locked`: cinematic means deliberate reveals, camera-like scale changes, layered depth, and controlled cropping
- `Locked`: playful but polished means small moments of charm through stickers or accents without disrupting the hero object
- `Locked`: product-focused means the product render is always the visual authority, not the typography or decoration

## Story Arc

### Scene 1: Anticipation

The visitor meets the hero coffee bean in a warm atmospheric environment. The goal is to create focus and tension.

### Scene 2: Transformation

The center tears open and the bean splits apart, turning anticipation into reveal. The goal is to make the transition feel authored rather than mechanical.

### Scene 3: Desire

The hero composition presents the product line as an object of taste and choice. The goal is to create immediate visual desire and brand recognition.

### Scene 4: Proof

One hero cup remains and carries the visitor into the freshness story. The goal is to shift from desire to credibility while keeping the product central.

## Scope Boundaries

`Locked`:

- single landing page only for v1
- no backend
- no ecommerce flow
- no CMS
- no multi-page architecture
- no sections after the initial freshness section

Current approved extension:

- add one downstream **Bestsellers** section after the freshness panel when explicitly requested
- keep it presentation-only and maintain the no-backend / no-checkout scope

## V1 Scene Scope

`Locked`: v1 includes the intro sequence, hero composition, and the opening freshness section after the pinned transition.

It does **not** include additional downstream sections beyond that first freshness panel.

Explicit user-request exception:

- the current implementation may include one **Bestsellers** follow-up section after freshness
